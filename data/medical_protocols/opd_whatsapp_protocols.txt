# OPD Clinical Protocols — Source: Institutional WhatsApp Notes
# Educational reference for MedRAG knowledge base. Clinician must verify before prescribing.

## Urinary Tract Infection (UTI)
- Non-pharmacological: Drink plenty of water
- Antibiotics according to culture/sensitivity when available
- Common regimens (uncomplicated cystitis, adult):
  - Levofloxacin 500-750 mg once daily x 7-10 days OR
  - Nitrofurantoin 100 mg twice daily x 7-10 days
- Adjunct: Alkasol syrup 2 tsp twice daily x 7 days
- Symptomatic: Flavoxate/Urispas 100 mg twice daily OR 200 mg once daily x 3-4 days

## Acute Gastroenteritis (AGE)
- Plenty of oral fluids, ORS
- Ciprofloxacin 500 mg twice daily x 5 days
- Metronidazole 400 mg three times daily x 5 days
- Probiotic: Econorm sachet once with half glass water twice daily x 14 days
- Paracetamol 650 mg as needed for fever
- Follow up at medical OPD/ER if worsening

## Common Antibiotic Reference (OPD)
- Azithromycin 500 mg once daily x 5 days
- Amoxicillin-clavulanate 625 mg three times daily x 5 days
- Doxycycline 100 mg twice daily x 7-10 days
- Nitrofurantoin 100 mg twice daily x 7-14 days
- Ciprofloxacin 500 mg twice daily x 5 days
- Metronidazole 400 mg three times daily x 5 days
- Cefixime 200 mg twice daily x 5 days
- Levofloxacin 500-750 mg once daily x 7 days

## Vertigo
- Betahistine (Vertin) 16 mg three times daily x 7 days
- Prochlorperazine (Stemetil-MD) 1 tab SOS for nausea/vomiting

## Lower Respiratory Tract Infection (LRTI) — Cough with expectoration and fever
- Amoxicillin-clavulanate 625 mg three times daily x 5 days
- Paracetamol 650 mg twice daily after food x 5 days
- Plenty of water
- Cough syrup: Ascoril-LS OR Grilinctus-BM 10 ml three times daily x 7 days
- Mucinac 600: 1 tab in half glass water twice daily x 3 days

## Secretory / Watery Diarrhoea
- Racecadotril 100 mg three times daily x 2 days (e.g. Zedott) — reduces stool frequency

## First Diagnosis Diabetes (RBS > 200)
- Metformin 500-1000 mg twice daily after food — continue
- Glimepiride 1 mg twice daily after food — continue
- Refer to diabetic/retina clinic

## Wheeze / Rhonchi (add-on)
- Duolin MDI 2 puffs three times daily — continue

## Sore Throat / Upper Respiratory Tract Infection (URTI)
- Azithromycin 500 mg once daily x 5 days
- Montelukast-Levocetirizine 10/5 mg once daily at bedtime x 10 days
- Multivitamin/B-complex twice daily after food x 14 days
- Plenty of water
- Chlorhexidine gargle three times daily x few days
- Paracetamol 650 mg twice daily after food x 5 days

## Severe Headache (migraine-type, no red flags)
- Naproxen/Domperidone combination (Naxdom 250/10) twice daily x 5 days
- Pantoprazole 40 mg once daily before breakfast x 1 month
- Amitriptyline 25 mg once daily x 3 months (migraine prophylaxis)

## Low Back Pain
- Ibuprofen (Brufen) 400 mg twice daily x 14 days
- Thiocolchicoside (FM) 20 mg twice daily after food x 14 days
- Calcium carbonate/Vitamin D (500/250) once daily after food — continue
- If radiation to lower limb: Methylcobalamin + Pregabalin (1500/75) once daily at bedtime x 1 month
- Branded alternative: Zerodol-MR twice daily after food x 14 days
- Advice: X-ray lumbosacral spine AP/Lateral; ortho OPD follow-up at 2 weeks

## Gastritis / Peptic Ulcer Disease
- Avoid spicy diet; small frequent meals
- Pantoprazole 40 mg once daily before breakfast x 4 weeks
- Drotaverine (Drotin) 40 mg SOS
- Sucralfate suspension 15 ml twice daily x 14 days (30 min before food)
- H. pylori suspected: H. pylori kit twice daily x 14 days

## Hypertension
- Emergency if > 180/110: Labetalol 20 mg IV slowly STAT; recheck BP after 1 hour
- Target range management 140/90 to 180/110:
  - Salt-restricted diet
  - Amlodipine 10 mg once daily before breakfast — continue
  - Pantoprazole 40 mg once daily before breakfast x 1 month
  - If anxiety: Clonazepam 0.25 mg once daily at bedtime x 10 days
